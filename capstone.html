Capstone Journal

May 5th

I went to training for the summer camp today. I had to wake up at 6:30 am and drive all the way to the UBC campus. The first thing we learned about was dealing with kids with exceptionalities. So, kids who have either learning disabilities, or other disabilities like hearing or sight. Later, we were put into groups and make a skit about what to do if there is an earthquake during the camp. After this, we were told that we had to create our own activity during our time at the science camp. I planned out a little workshop where I would lead the kids through making a simple random 8-ball fortune teller. I was going to do this on the last day of the science camp. We went over some scenarios of what could happen, and how to deal with those situations. I plan to make sure I practice the coding workshop, so I don’t mess up during the presentation. I will also review the instructor manual to make sure I know everything.

June 2nd

It was the first day of the camp today. We had to be at the school 2 hours before all the kids showed up. We went over the shape of the day, and whether any of the kids coming had any exceptionalities. There were some. I helped the teachers cut up some pipe cleaners for the first activity. Which was growing salt crystals. I was mostly with one group that needed the most help. The other 3 volunteers who were with me were very nice. The four of us also had to prepare a workshop with the kids together. We would do this on Thursday. We haven’t decided what we will do yet. The day went by simply. We were told today would be a “chill” day, as we would just be getting to know the kids and getting them comfortable with us. The kids were all being nice, there were a couple that I thought would be trouble. They all had to log in to some computers and follow an interactive tutorial about volcanoes at the end of the day. I helped a lot of the kids with that.

June 3rd

Second day was more stressful. Like yesterday I helped the instructors with setup for the activities we would do today. The first activity was about colors. We made a color wheel which would turn white when we spun it really fast. We were told to be extra careful to make sure the kids don’t hurt themselves with the scissors. At recess I was supervising kids on the playground. Once back inside, the kids made refrigerators with tinfoil, paper, Styrofoam and felt cloth. They had to put an ice cube inside and try to keep it as ice for as long as possible. As usual, I hovered around the class and helped those who asked for help and tried to give inspiration to those were not doing anything. Then it was lunch time. I played basketball with the kids from the older. There was a problem with some kids feeling left out. So I decided to remake the teams. Since I was taller and older than the kids there, I was at a very large advantage. I took the kids who were feeling left out and made sure they were playing while on my team. Everyone had way more fun this way. Once back inside, the kids continued with their refrigerators. I took my 30 minutes break here, I practiced my workshop I was going to present in a couple of days. Then when it was time to go home, I helped empty the kid’s refrigerators and made sure their parents picked them up. Then did some cleanup with the instructors and went home.

June 4th

Third day, and tomorrow is my presentation. I worked on it a lot yesterday evening to make sure I could explain everything well and made sure that I had answers to some questions that might come up. I wrote down some notes on some things to keep in mind. The first activity was a zipline activity. The kids made little carriers that were to be put on a string and ziplined down on an incline. I handled the hot glue for the class, because the kids were too young to handle hot glue guns. At recess there were problems on the playground. One kid had an argument with another kid and sat on the swings the whole time and wouldn’t come back inside. I did what was written in the junior instructor manual and first told the teachers and followed their instructions. I tried my best to convince him to come back inside. It took a while, but in the end, I said we would play basketball, with just me and him on a team. That seemed to cheer him up and he came back inside. Inside, the class worked on their ziplines and finally tested them out on the string. I was holding up the zipline and was giving more suggestions on how they could improve on their carriers. Lunch went by smoothly, there weren’t any conflicts and I played basketball with the kid from earlier. Inside, the teachers chose to play a game with the kids. They asked me to lead it, so I chose to play hot potato. They had a lot of fun, and it was almost time to go home. The kids went home while a magic school bus show was running, and the class was empty in about 15 mins. I stayed back and helped clean up for 1 more hour before I went home.

June 5th What I presented

Fourth day, and today was presentation day. When I went in for preparation of the day, I went over my presentation with the teachers. They said it was great. The kids came in on time and were given a riddle to keep them occupied while we checked in the rest of the kids. The kids were making model volcanoes with clay. We had pre-cut the clay in the morning into slices for the kids and encouraged them to be creative. I gave them a quick lesson on different types of volcanoes and the teachers gave the instructions. Most students took a similar approach of a pointy volcano, but some made a flatter shield volcano. Recess was the best it has ever been, there were no issues and I played basketball with the boys. Back inside, the kids finished off their volcanoes and we would erupt them after lunch. Lunch went by smoothly as well. Now it was time to erupt their volcanoes. We did it outside and it got messy. It took about 10 minutes. Now it was time for my presentation. I first started off by showing them the finished product and “read some of their fortunes”. They all seemed to love it and since they were 7 years old… they believed it. I got a lot of questions along the way about loops. Loops are blocks of code that repeat themselves with small iterations. I took some time to go through what loops and conditional statements were before continuing. I had the kids vote on the appearance of the project. They chose for a witch theme. Once it was finished one of the kids volunteered to ask a question. He asked, “Am I cool?”. I run the fortune teller, and of course, the universe chose “Of course not!”. The kid started crying, but I quickly told him I pressed the button wrong, so I did it again and he got “Yes”. The kids quickly learned not to ask questions about them selves if they didn’t want to get heart broken. They seemed to enjoy it and they all played with it for a while.

June 6th

Yesterday was my last day at camp. During the end of yesterday the whole staff and junior instructors sat in a circle and wrote down 3 people in and something they really noticed about them. That was a nice experience because it really felt like I was appreciated for my hard work. I don’t have any regrets of volunteering here but I wished I had kept more detailed notes on what I did throughout the day. At the end it became a bit of a blur because of how tired I was in the end. I was the only one playing sports with the kids and that also caused me to be fatigued. I hope to volunteer here next year as well. I plan do my capstone on this, and I think it will really help me find a job in software engineering.
